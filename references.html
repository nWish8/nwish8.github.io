My Thesis

My thesis focused on optimizing energy-efficient routing protocols in wireless sensor networks (WSNs) for smart agriculture, specifically tailored to vineyard environments. The research addressed challenges such as power efficiency, data reliability, and adaptive routing in remote outdoor settings. By leveraging node metrics like battery level, signal strength, distance, and priority, I developed an adaptive routing algorithm to optimize energy consumption in scalable networks.

This work contributes to the field of smart agriculture by presenting a scalable, cost-effective WSN model for vineyards, reducing energy overhead and supporting sustainable agricultural practices.
